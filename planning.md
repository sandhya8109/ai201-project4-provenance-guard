# Provenance Guard: System Planning & Architecture

## Architecture 

**Submission Flow:** A creator submits their work via the `POST /submit` endpoint. The text is passed simultaneously to Signal 1 (Groq LLM) for semantic analysis and Signal 2 (Python Stylometrics - Type-Token Ratio) for vocabulary diversity calculation. The raw outputs from both signals are fed into the Confidence Scoring module, which weights them and outputs a single calibrated score between 0.0 and 1.0. This score determines the correct Transparency Label. Finally, the entire decision—including individual signal scores, final confidence score, and content ID—is written to the Audit Log, and the system returns the structured JSON response to the user.

**Appeal Flow:** If a creator believes their work was falsely flagged, they hit the `POST /appeal` endpoint with their reasoning and `content_id`. The system updates the content's status to "under review", appends the creator's reasoning to the Audit Log, and returns a confirmation.

 1. Detection Signals
Signal 1: Groq LLM (Llama-3)

What it measures: Semantic coherence, narrative structure, and holistic style.

Output: A float between 0.0 (Definitely Human) and 1.0 (Definitely AI).

Blind spot: Highly structured, formal human writing (like an academic abstract or legal brief) often gets flagged as AI because it naturally lacks emotional quirks and uses formulaic transitions.

Signal 2: Stylometrics (Type-Token Ratio / TTR)

What it measures: Vocabulary diversity (number of unique words divided by total words). AI models heavily favor probable next words, leading to a narrower vocabulary, whereas humans use niche or varied words.

Output: A float between 0.0 and 1.0 representing the ratio. For scoring, we will invert this (1.0 - TTR) so that a higher number equals a higher AI probability.

Blind spot: Very short simple texts (like a quick text message or flash fiction) naturally have low vocabulary diversity, which might falsely flag as AI.

2. Uncertainty Representation & Confidence Scoring
A score of 0.5 to our system does not mean a binary flip; it represents genuine uncertainty. Because a false positive (labeling human work as AI) is worse than a false negative, the "Uncertain" safety net is mathematically wide.

Scoring Logic:

Formula: Final Confidence = (Groq_Score * 0.70) + (Inverted_TTR * 0.30)

We weight the LLM heavier because TTR can be skewed by text length.

Thresholds:

0.00 to 0.35: Likely Human.

0.36 to 0.75: Uncertain / Mixed Signals. System errs on the side of caution.

0.76 to 1.00: Likely AI-Generated.

3. Transparency Label Design
Depending on the final confidence score, the user will see one of the following verbatim labels:

High-Confidence Human (0.00 - 0.35): "Likely Human. Our detection signals indicate with high confidence that this content is organically written."

Uncertain (0.36 - 0.75): "Unclassified. This content exhibits mixed signals. Our system cannot definitively determine whether it was written by a human or generated by AI."

High-Confidence AI (0.76 - 1.00): "Likely AI-Generated. Our detection signals indicate with high confidence that this content was primarily generated by artificial intelligence. If you believe this is an error, please submit an appeal."

4. Appeals Workflow
Who can submit: The original creator of the content.

What information they provide: The content_id and a creator_reasoning text field.

System actions: Intercepts the request, changes the specific submission's status from classified to under_review, appends the creator's reasoning to the Audit Log, and returns a JSON confirmation.

Human reviewer view: A moderator opening the queue sees the raw text, Groq score, TTR score, combined confidence score, and the creator's written explanation to make a final manual decision.

5. Anticipated Edge Cases
Extremely Short Creative Works (e.g., Haikus or Flash Fiction): Because TTR requires a decent sample size of words to accurately calculate vocabulary diversity, very short texts will skew the math. This will artificially inflate the AI-likelihood score.

Highly Formal, Technical, or Academic Writing: Human writing that naturally lacks emotional variance and follows rigid structural formulas will likely trigger the Groq LLM's semantic flags. The LLM might misinterpret this intentional formality as AI-generated neutrality.
